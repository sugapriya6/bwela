Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. It is a deep blue ice giant with strong winds and storms. Neptune's largest moon, Triton, is geologically active and may have a subsurface ocean. It was discovered in 1846 and is the only planet not visible to the naked eye.

Atmosphere: Neptune is an ice giant with an atmosphere of hydrogen, helium, and methane.

Water/Ice: Water exists mostly as ice inside the planet.

Other Facts: Known for the fastest winds in the solar system. Triton, its largest moon, may have a subsurface ocean. Discovered in 1846.
